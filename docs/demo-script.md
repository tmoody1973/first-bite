# First Bite — Demo Video Script (3:30)

> AI voiceover via Kite.video. Screen recording — real-time, no mockups.
> Rule: Show the "wow" immediately. No long intro. Every second earns its place.

---

## SCENE 1: THE WOW — SHOW IT WORKING (0:00–0:15)

**SCREEN:** App is already loaded. Type "Street food in Lagos" and hit "Bon Voyage." Loading screen appears with pipeline steps.

**VO:**
"One sentence. Eight modalities. Watch."

> **PRODUCTION NOTE:** No intro. No logo reveal. Start with the prompt being typed. The first thing judges see is the agent working.

---

## SCENE 2: THE PITCH — WHILE IT GENERATES (0:15–0:35)

**SCREEN:** Loading screen — stops checking off: Stop 1 generating, Stop 2 generating...

**VO:**
"If you want to explore the food culture of a place before you visit, your options are food blogs stuffed with stock photos or a chatbot that gives you a wall of text. Nobody is building what food travel actually feels like — the sound of the market, the history of the neighborhood, the recipe a grandmother would recognize. First Bite is an AI storytelling agent that turns one sentence into a complete immersive food journey. Text, images, audio, video, real restaurants, real recipes. All generated by Gemini in one flow."

---

## SCENE 3: THE EXPERIENCE REVEALS (0:35–1:15)

**SCREEN:** Journey reveals — Stop 1 full-screen. Scene image as background. TTS narration auto-plays. Ambient market sounds audible underneath. Photo carousel cycling.

**VO:**
"Five stops. Each one is a single Gemini call with interleaved output — text and images composed together, not stitched after the fact. The narrator voice is Gemini TTS. That ambient sound — the crowd, the sizzling oil — is ElevenLabs, generated from a text description of this specific scene. The photos rotate between the AI-generated scene, the actual Google Street View, and a real photo from Google Places with the restaurant's rating."

> **PRODUCTION NOTE:** Let the app's audio play under the voiceover. Judges should hear the narration and ambient sound — that's proof the audio features work.

---

## SCENE 4: THE HISTORY (1:15–1:35)

**SCREEN:** Navigate to Stop 2 or 3. Point at the narrative text on screen.

**VO:**
"The narrative isn't generic. It tells you when this market was built, who the vendors are, what trade routes brought these spices here. Google Search grounding verifies the history and the restaurants. Every fact the agent states, it checked."

---

## SCENE 5: THE RECIPE (1:35–1:55)

**SCREEN:** Tap dish thumbnail. Recipe card slides up — two-column layout, dish photo, ingredients, steps. Tap "Save" — PNG downloads.

**VO:**
"Every stop has a full home-cookable recipe. The dish photo is Nano Banana 2. The recipe tells you where the dish came from — not just how to cook it, but why it exists. Tap save, you get a downloadable recipe card."

---

## SCENE 6: THE PLACE (1:55–2:10)

**SCREEN:** Show place card with rating. Tap "View on Map." Maps modal opens.

**VO:**
"Every restaurant is verified through Google Places. Real name, real address, real rating. This isn't hallucinated. Grounding keeps it honest."

---

## SCENE 7: THE FINALE (2:10–2:35)

**SCREEN:** Navigate to the last slide. Veo video plays full-screen behind the vintage travel poster. Show the poster details — all five dishes, all five restaurants.

**VO:**
"The last slide. That video is Veo 3.1 — an eight-second cinematic recap of the journey, generated from descriptions of all five stops. The poster is Nano Banana — vintage French lithograph style with every dish and restaurant. You can download it. Share the whole journey with a link — anyone can view it."

> **PRODUCTION NOTE:** This is the "wow" closer. Make sure the Veo video is playing. Hold on this shot for a beat.

---

## SCENE 8: THE DASHBOARD (2:35–2:45)

**SCREEN:** Navigate to dashboard. Show journey gallery with poster cards. Show the interactive map.

**VO:**
"Every journey saved. An interactive map that grows with each trip. Your collection of food stories from around the world."

---

## SCENE 9: THE ARCHITECTURE (2:45–3:10)

**SCREEN:** Architecture diagram. Then quick flash: Cloud Run console (live service), Firestore (journey docs), Cloud Storage bucket (images/audio/video). 2-3 seconds each.

**VO:**
"Four Gemini models. 3.1 Flash for interleaved text and images. 3.1 Pro as orchestrator. 2.5 Flash TTS for narration. Veo for video. Google Search grounding on every call. Google Places, Street View, and Geocoding for real-world data. ElevenLabs for ambient sound. All on Cloud Run with Firestore and Cloud Storage. The deploy script is in the repo — one command stands up the whole stack. You're looking at the live Cloud Run service right now."

> **PRODUCTION NOTE:** Flash the Cloud Run console for 2-3 seconds. This is "visual proof of Cloud deployment."

---

## SCENE 10: THE CLOSE (3:10–3:25)

**SCREEN:** Back to landing page. URL appears.

**VO:**
"One sentence. Eight modalities. Text, images, real photos, Street View, ambient sound, narration, video, and maps. A food journey worth taking."

## BEFORE YOU RECORD

1. **Pre-generate a "Street food in Lagos" journey** so the experience loads fast during recording
2. **Make sure the Veo video loaded** on the final slide
3. **Test that ambient sound and TTS narration play** — judges need to hear them
4. **Open Cloud Run console in a separate tab** for the quick flash in Scene 9
5. **Have the architecture diagram ready** as an image
6. **Record at 1920x1080, 60fps**
7. **No background music** — the app provides its own audio
8. **Rehearse the script once** reading it aloud — check that it fits 3:25
